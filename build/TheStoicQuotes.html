The quotes are organized into four different categories: Perseverance, Wisdom, Happiness, and Self-Control for easy browsing. Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius was a Roman Emperor and a Stoic philosopher. He is best known for his "Meditations", a collection of personal reflections on Stoic philosophy and the challenges of leadership.

Epictetus

Epictetus was a Greek philosopher who was born a slave but later freed. He is known for his practical and straightforward approach to Stoicism, emphasizing the importance of self-control and inner peace.

Seneca

Seneca was a Roman statesman and playwright who also wrote extensively on Stoic philosophy. He emphasized the importance of living in accordance with nature and the idea of "amor fati" or love of fate.

Zeno of Citium

Zeno was a Greek philosopher and the founder of Stoicism. He is known for his emphasis on the idea of "virtue is sufficient for happiness".

Cleanthes

Cleanthes was a Greek philosopher and the second head of the Stoic school of philosophy. He was a disciple of Zeno and is known for his hymn to Zeus, which emphasizes the Stoic idea of the unity of all things.

Chrysippus

Chrysippus was a Greek philosopher and the third head of the Stoic school of philosophy. He is known for his contributions to Stoic logic and his vast body of work, which helped to establish Stoicism as a systematic philosophy.

Diogenes of Sinope

Diogenes was a Greek philosopher and a contemporary of Plato and Aristotle. He was known for his extreme asceticism and his rejection of traditional social norms, which earned him the nickname "the Cynic".

Cato the Younger

Cato was a Roman statesman and a prominent Stoic philosopher. He is known for his strong principles and his refusal to compromise, even in the face of political opposition.

Musonius Rufus

Musonius was a Roman philosopher and a prominent teacher of Stoicism. He emphasized the importance of practical philosophy and the application of Stoic principles to everyday life.

Epiphany of Phrygia

Epiphany was a Stoic philosopher and a prominent teacher of Stoicism in Phrygia. He is known for his emphasis on the idea of the "virtuous circle", which holds that virtuous actions lead to virtuous thoughts and vice versa.
